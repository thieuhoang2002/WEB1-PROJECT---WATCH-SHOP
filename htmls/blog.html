The Royal Oak Concept Flying Tourbillon GMT watch presents itself in an innovative green case

Always taking celebrations seriously, in fact, the Concept line was created by the Swiss brand as a special project to mark the 30th birthday of the Royal Oak in 2002. Since then, the Concept line has acts as the most avant-garde of the Royal Oak collection, with very limited releases, but certainly bold modern designs with groundbreaking technology inside.

The Royal Oak Concept Flying Tourbillon GMT was first introduced in 2018, but the general character of the line is derived from the Concept GMT Tourbillon (not flying) version of 2011. The latest version has been completed with flying tourbillon cage. Enclosing the watch is a 44mm x 16.1mm blue ceramic case, sapphire crystal front and back revealing the movements of both the dial and the movement.

Super rare Rolex Pink Milgauss 6541 Appears at Upcoming Antiquorum House Auction!

The Milgauss was the first watch to have a case containing a wrought iron ring surrounding the movement. Based on the principles of the Faraday cage, the case structure protected the movement from magnetic influences, which set the Milgauss apart from other models of the time.

Milgauss was developed with a clear purpose: to serve in a specialized environment at a time when industrial progress was rapid. Made by Rolex in 1958, the Milgauss is a watch for engineers, doctors, and scientists forced to spend a lot of time working in power plants and other facilities with strong magnetic fields.

It is believed that Rolex produces only about 200 Milgauss with model number 6541, one of the good reasons why watches are so rare and so attractive to true collectors. In addition to the wrought iron ring surrounding the movement, the Milgauss 6541 features a classic ancestral dial, a rotatable bezel, and a lightning-shaped seconds hand. This combination gives the watch a sporty look, but not to be confused with the Submariner of the same period.

At the first stage, the Milgauss inscription on the dial is red, matching the red triangle indication on the bezel. However, as time went on, the lettering faded to pink, further adding to the vintage element of this watch. And that is also the reason why this watch is also called “Pink Milgauss” by auction houses.
